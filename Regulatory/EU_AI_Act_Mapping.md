### **Counter-Spy.ai: EU AI Act Compliance Mapping**


### **Executive Summary for the Compliance Officer**

**Counter-Spy.ai acts as a "Compliance Wrapper."** By placing Counter-Spy between the user and the LLM, the organization instantly gains the **Human Oversight (Art. 14)**, **Cybersecurity Robustness (Art. 15)**, and **Record-Keeping (Art. 12)** capabilities legally required to operate an enterprise generative AI system within the European Union.

#### **1. Article 14: Human Oversight**
*The EU AI Act mandates that high-risk AI systems must be designed in a way that allows them to be effectively overseen by natural persons, including the ability to intervene, disregard, or completely halt the system.*
* **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Mode:** Counter-Spy automatically intercepts borderline traffic (e.g., suspicious entropy >4.5) and routes it to a `PENDING_REVIEW` queue, fulfilling the mandate that humans must be able to review and intervene before an automated action executes.
* **Global System Pause (DEFCON 1):** The Act specifically requires a "stop button" or equivalent mechanism to safely halt operations. Counter-Spyâ€™s DEFCON 1 toggle instantly halts 100% of automated inference and routes everything to manual review during an attack, perfectly satisfying this requirement.
* **Analyst Review Workflow:** Allows authorized personnel to override the system, update the `resultantSeverity` of logs, and actively manage the AI's boundaries.

#### **2. Article 15: Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity**
*The Act requires AI systems to be resilient against errors, faults, and malicious actions (like adversarial attacks, prompt injections, and data poisoning).*
* **Defense-in-Depth Sanitization (Regex, Entropy, Complexity):** By blocking prompt injections, token dilution attacks, and obfuscated payloads before they hit the LLM, Counter-Spy enforces the required cybersecurity resilience against adversarial manipulation.
* **Output Sanitization Layer:** Prevents the system from failing into an unsafe state by scanning and neutralizing toxic or leaked information generated by the model.
* **Anti-ReDoS Circuit Breaker:** Fulfils the robustness requirement by protecting the application from algorithmic Denial of Service (DoS) attacks designed to exhaust system resources.
* **Dependency Governance:** Pinning React versions and isolating Vite mitigates upstream supply chain vulnerabilities, maintaining the structural cybersecurity of the environment.

#### **3. Article 12: Record-Keeping (Logging)**
*High-risk systems must automatically record events (logs) throughout their lifecycle to ensure traceability, monitor anomalies, and facilitate post-incident investigations.*
* **Advanced Audit Trail:** The platform logs every interaction via a strict Firestore schema (capturing `userId`, `sessionId`, `timestamp`, `entropy`, and `detectionFlags`). 
* **Anomaly Detection Dashboard:** Features real-time Z-Score calculations to track the velocity of threats, satisfying the requirement to monitor and log system performance and anomalies continuously.
* **Full Prompt Inspection:** Retains the exact prompts and AI responses, allowing regulators and internal auditors to trace the exact lineage of an AI interaction.

#### **4. Article 10: Data and Data Governance (and GDPR Alignment)**
*AI systems must be trained and operated using data subject to appropriate data governance and management practices, specifically regarding privacy and bias.*
* **Local PII & Secret Redaction:** By stripping SSNs, emails, phone numbers, and keys *before* the data leaves the client environment, Counter-Spy enforces **Data Minimization** (a core tenet of both the AI Act and GDPR), ensuring unauthorized personal data never enters the LLM's context window.
* **Automated Golden Set Refinement (DPO):** The Act requires training data to be relevant, representative, and free of errors. The Golden Set feature allows analysts to carefully curate and export highly structured, reviewed interactions for future fine-tuning, ensuring the downstream model's training data meets governance standards.

#### **5. Article 9: Risk Management System**
*Deployers must establish a continuous iterative process to identify, estimate, and evaluate risks that the AI system may pose to health, safety, or fundamental rights.*
* **Dynamic Guardrails:** Administrators can toggle security features (Blocked Keywords, Forbidden Topics) on the fly. This allows the organization to dynamically adjust the AIâ€™s risk posture as new threats are identified.
* **Forbidden Topics Enforcement:** By strictly prohibiting the model from discussing restricted subjects (e.g., specific political or financial topics), the organization mitigates the risk of the AI violating fundamental rights or generating harmful misinformation.
